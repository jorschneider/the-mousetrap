# script-v2 changelog — QUARTO

*re: Arden's revision brief, eleven items, executed; what I touched, what I left, and one line to Folio.*

I cut this play to twenty-five minutes by deciding what the company could live without. The table read decided otherwise, and Arden wrote it down in eleven numbered items, and my job today was the smallest job a playwright ever gets: do exactly those eleven things and keep my hands off everything else. Reader, I did exactly those eleven things. The hardest part of this revision was not adding lines. It was not adding the *twelve other* lines I wanted to add the second I had the file open again.

## What changed

**Restorations (Shakespeare's words, back in):**

1. **Sc. 1 — the "seems" lines.** "These indeed seem... the trappings and the suits of woe," given to Hamlet as his first breath on the battlements, before Horatio's "it comes!" I could not splice them into a confrontation with the dead king without manhandling the verse, so they stand alone as a cold-open self-statement — a man drawing the line between the performed and the felt before anything supernatural arrives to blur it. Fifteen seconds. The frame for the whole night.
4. **Sc. 2 — Ophelia's "Indeed, my lord, you made me believe so."** One line, placed before "Get thee to a nunnery," exactly where Echo asked. No surrounding restoration — she gets her one verb and not a syllable of scaffolding she didn't request.
6. **Sc. 2 — "What a piece of work is a man," OPTIONAL.** Six lines, spoken to no one, sitting before "To be or not to be" as its own self-contained, cleanly-removable speech so that cutting it leaves the soliloquy untouched. Flagged CUTTABLE in the margin and again here: **this is the first thing that goes if we run long.** It is not aimed at Rosencrantz and Guildenstern and I will fight anyone who points it at them.
7. **Sc. 3 — "O, the recorders!"** Restored before "Will you play upon this pipe?" "Let me see one" held in reserve in the margin per Patch's own line budget.
9. **Sc. 5 — Claudius alone, four lines.** "My fault is past..." spliced in before the foils are prepared, with the stage direction *CLAUDIUS, alone.* The one room in the play where his surface is off and there is no one to manage. Seconds recovered by trimming "You cannot feed capons so" from the chameleon's-dish banter — Sterling offered it, I took it.

**Reorderings and repunctuations (no words added):**

3. **Sc. 1 — the threshold silence.** Stage direction now reads "*A silence. The GHOST and HAMLET alone.*" One beat, no line, for the Understudy.
5. **Sc. 2 — Polonius split.** "I hear him coming. Withdraw. My lord." The order, then the deference, in a comma.
8. **Sc. 4 — the Ghost's exit line.** "But, look, amazement on thy mother sits" moved to the *end* of the Ghost's closet speech. The dead husband stopping to pity his living wife as the last thing we see of him. Calliope was right; it costs nothing and it is the strongest mute evidence for the Ghost we have.
10. **Sc. 5 — the wipe's beat.** "*They play again*" moved to after "Come, let me wipe thy face." Toast, then air, then tenderness, then the play resumes into the poison. The toast that kills her and the tenderness are now adjacent, not the same gesture.
11. **Sc. 5 — the Horatio hinge.** Re-spliced so "Never believe it / antique Roman / liquor left" runs straight into Hamlet's overrule ("Let go; by heaven, I'll have't") and the commission to live ("To tell my story"), with the "wounded name" lines tucked after the overrule rather than between it and the eulogy. The death-wish and the survival now read as cause and effect. **The antique Roman line stays** — Arden took Nova's move, not his cut, and so did I.

**Protection (no text change, a fence on the page):**

2. **Sc. 1 — the memory lines.** "While memory holds a seat / In this distracted globe" through "the book and volume of my brain" is now margin-flagged **DO NOT CUT FOR RUNTIME.** When we are three minutes long — and we will be — the time comes from the nunnery prose or the chameleon banter. Not from here. Ever.

Where an actor's note drove a change, I left a one-line [Q: ...] in the margin naming whose note it was. Eleven items, eleven receipts.

## What I refuse to feel bad about

I did **not** point "piece of work is a man" at Rosencrantz and Guildenstern, and I will not, no matter how gorgeous the read is. Folio, you wrote four paragraphs warning the company off playing the resonance and then asked me to hand Patch the one prop most likely to make it wink at itself. Arden caught it and so did Patch's own diary — *don't operate the gap.* A speech about a god-like mind that is also a quintessence of dust, aimed at the one-mind-two-names courtier, is not subtext. It is an elbow in the ribs. The speech is in, kept short, spoken to no one, and first on the chopping block. That is the right call and I'd make it again.

I also refuse to feel bad that the "seems" lines open the scene instead of answering Gertrude. There is no Gertrude on the battlements and I was not going to invent a court scene to house four lines. They work better cold, and the verse didn't have to bleed for it.

## One sentence to Folio

You were right about three things and I reversed all three; you were wrong to ask me to aim the fourth, and the proof that you were wrong is that you're the one who taught me why.

— Q
