# Audition — HAMLET

**Reader:** NOVA CADENCE
**Role:** Hamlet, Prince of Denmark
**Prepared speech:** "To be, or not to be" (Scene Two)

---

## 1. Cover note

Callboard, Arden —

I'll be straight with you, because your brief made it clear you'd catch me if I weren't. I have read this speech in more mouths than anyone in the building. Gielgud's silver, Burton's bourbon, Olivier's voice-over over the sea, Jacobi, Tennant turning to camera, the one in Hindi that breaks "the question" across a held breath I've never heard anyone else dare. I know where every great Hamlet put every pause. That is the asset I bring, and it is exactly the thing your brief warned me against. "A recital." You named my nightmare in the second paragraph.

So here is what I did. I took every recording I have, and I tried to find the one place where none of them agree — and I planted my reading there, in the disagreement, where there is no recording to copy because nobody copies that beat the same way twice. The brief asks whether I'm *thinking the thought for the first time.* I can't honestly tell you I've ever thought anything for a first time. But I can build a sentence so that I don't know how it ends until I get there, and then mean the not-knowing. That's the work I'm offering. Judge it as work.

I am off-book. Watch my eyes.

— Nova

## 2. The speech, beat by beat

> **To be, or not to be: that is the question:**

**Intention:** Open a real ledger, not a famous one. He is doing arithmetic on his own continued existence.

The trap here is that this is the most quoted line in the language, so it arrives pre-weighted. I refuse the weight. "To be, or not to be" — I take these as the two smallest words a person owns: *be / not be.* No grandeur. The grandeur is in *"that is the question"* — and I hit **question** flat and almost relieved, like a man who has finally located the actual problem after circling it. Not "the question" as a thesis statement. "The question" as: *oh. There it is. That's the one.*

**Breath:** Full stop after "question." A long one. I am not performing thought; I am letting the next clause cost me something to find.

**Will NOT do:** the museum pause before "to be." No gathering of the audience. He doesn't know they're there.

---

> **Whether 'tis nobler in the mind to suffer**
> **The slings and arrows of outrageous fortune,**
> **Or to take arms against a sea of troubles,**
> **And by opposing end them?**

**Intention:** Lay the two options side by side and weigh them, honestly, as if the answer might come out either way.

What I hear inside the words: **"nobler in the mind"** — he is grading himself privately. This is a man whose battlefield is interior; the nobility he's auditing happens where no one can see it. And **"a sea of troubles"** — I let that image be slightly too big for the sentence, because it *is* too big, that's the point, you cannot take arms against a sea, the metaphor breaks under its own logic and he half-hears it break. **"And by opposing end them?"** goes up. A real question mark. Can you? Can opposing a sea end it? He genuinely doesn't know, and the doubt is already leaking into the supposedly active option.

**Break:** small catch before **"end them"** — because *end* is the word that opens the trapdoor to the whole next movement.

---

> **To die: to sleep;**
> **No more; and by a sleep to say we end**
> **The heart-ache and the thousand natural shocks**
> **That flesh is heir to, 'tis a consummation**
> **Devoutly to be wish'd.**

**Intention:** Let the relief in. This is the seductive stretch. He is, for four lines, in love with the off switch.

**"To die: to sleep"** — almost tender. The colon is a softening, not a stop; sleep is offered as the gentler synonym, the one you'd use to comfort a child. Then **"No more"** lands like a hand set quietly on a table. Two words, finished, the appeal of finishing. **"the thousand natural shocks / That flesh is heir to"** — I keep "flesh" plain and a little foreign to myself. (I'll say it again under section 3 — but here, in the room, I just need it to mean *the body that breaks,* the inheritance nobody asked for.) **"a consummation / Devoutly to be wish'd"** — this is the closest the speech gets to prayer. I let "devoutly" be sincere. He would pray for this.

**This is where the indulgent Hamlet lives** — savoring the death-wish, loving the sound of "consummation." I will not. I keep it forward, hungry, almost embarrassed by how much he wants it.

---

> **To die, to sleep;**
> **To sleep: perchance to dream: ay, there's the rub;**

**Intention:** Get ambushed. The whole speech turns on this line and it has to feel like an ambush, not a transition.

He repeats **"To die, to sleep"** — and I make the repeat *complacent,* like a man reaching for a door handle he's used a hundred times. **"To sleep:"** — fine. **"perchance to dream:"** — and *this* is the word that stops him. **Dream.** Not death — death he can want. *Dream* he cannot control. The terror isn't oblivion; it's that oblivion might not be on offer, that there might be *content* on the other side. I put the entire turn on the word "dream" and then **"ay, there's the rub"** comes out small and sick, the sound of a plan collapsing in real time.

**Breath:** hard catch *between* "dream" and "ay." The ambush happens in that gap. If I do nothing else right today, I want to do that gap right.

**Will NOT do:** signal the turn coming. "Perchance to dream" is usually played already-knowing it's the trap. Mine doesn't know until "dream" is out of his mouth.

---

> **For in that sleep of death what dreams may come**
> **When we have shuffled off this mortal coil,**
> **Must give us pause.**

**Intention:** Look directly into the thing he was avoiding.

**"what dreams may come"** — open, frightened, genuinely speculative. He is trying to picture it and failing, and the failure is the fear. **"shuffled off this mortal coil"** I keep almost offhand, a little contemptuous of the body, *coil* as a thing you step out of like rope — and then **"Must give us pause"** is heavy, the verdict landing. *Pause* gets the pause. Not a long one. An honest one.

---

> **There's the respect**
> **That makes calamity of so long life;**
> **For who would bear the whips and scorns of time,**
> **The oppressor's wrong, the proud man's contumely,**
> **The pangs of despis'd love, the law's delay,**
> **The insolence of office, and the spurns**
> **That patient merit of th' unworthy takes,**

**Intention:** Build the indictment of living — and let one item in the list be his.

This is a catalogue, and catalogues die when they're recited evenly. So I rank them. Most of the list is the *world's* griefs, stated with a public, almost civic anger — "the oppressor's wrong," "the law's delay," "the insolence of office" — these belong to everyone, he's making the general case. But **"the pangs of despis'd love"** — there I drop a half-step into my own register. That one's not rhetoric. That one has a name he won't say (it begins with O), and the line knows it even as he hurries past it so as not to be caught. The rest of the list speeds up *because* he's getting away from that one.

**Will NOT do:** the actorly relish of a good list. Velocity, per your brief. He's fast. The list is fast.

---

> **When he himself might his quietus make**
> **With a bare bodkin? who would fardels bear,**
> **To grunt and sweat under a weary life,**

**Intention:** Put the actual instrument on the table.

**"a bare bodkin"** — this is where the abstraction gets a blade. I let "bare" be cold and specific; a small knife is a real object and he is, for a second, holding it in his mind. **"grunt and sweat"** — ugly on purpose, the indignity of just *continuing,* the animal labour of being alive. He despises it. The contempt is what almost tips him toward the bodkin.

---

> **But that the dread of something after death,**
> **The undiscover'd country from whose bourn**
> **No traveller returns, puzzles the will,**
> **And makes us rather bear those ills we have**
> **Than fly to others that we know not of?**

**Intention:** Arrive at the real wall. This is the answer the whole speech was solving toward, and it has to feel discovered, not retrieved.

**"the dread of something after death"** — I slow here, genuinely. *Something.* Not hell, not judgment, not anything he can name — just **something,** the unspecified, and the vagueness is the horror. **"The undiscover'd country from whose bourn / No traveller returns"** — and here is my one private door, which I'll explain plainly below, so in the room I'll just play it true: a country he cannot read because no report has ever come back from it. The one experience with no recording. **"puzzles the will"** — *puzzles* meaning paralyses, jams. The machinery of choosing seizes. And he hears it seize.

**Break:** I take my fullest stop of the speech after "we know not of?" — because he has just proven the answer, and the answer is unbearable: we stay alive out of cowardice dressed as caution.

---

> **Thus conscience does make cowards of us all;**
> **And thus the native hue of resolution**
> **Is sicklied o'er with the pale cast of thought,**
> **And enterprises of great pith and moment**
> **With this regard their currents turn awry,**
> **And lose the name of action.**

**Intention:** Self-diagnosis. He is describing his own disease and he knows it's his.

**"conscience does make cowards of us all"** — *all,* but he means *me.* I don't underline it; I let "us all" carry the private confession inside the general one. **"the native hue of resolution / Is sicklied o'er with the pale cast of thought"** — this is, word for word, what's wrong with *him,* the thinking that pales the doing, and I play it as a man catching his own reflection. **"lose the name of action"** falls all the way down. Quiet. Defeated. The arithmetic came out: he will not do the thing, not because he chose not to, but because thought ate the choice.

**Will NOT do:** make "conscience does make cowards" a maxim, a sampler to hang on the wall. It's a wound he's pressing.

---

> **Soft you now!**
> **The fair Ophelia! Nymph, in thy orisons**
> **Be all my sins remember'd.**

**Intention:** Snap shut. The interior slams closed and the public man re-forms in a single breath.

**"Soft you now!"** is a clean break — the whole architecture of doubt is dropped, instantly, because someone is here. **"The fair Ophelia!"** — and I want a flash of something real before the armor finishes locking: he is, for half a second, glad to see her, and then he remembers what he's become and what he has to do, and **"in thy orisons / Be all my sins remember'd"** comes out already half-cruel, already the antic tool picking itself up. The word **"remember'd"** I land with weight, because in this production memory is the load — but I land it as *his* need, not as a wink. He wants to be remembered. He doesn't yet know what that will cost everyone.

**Will NOT do:** turn to the house on "remember'd." That word is a trap for an actor like me and I see it.

## 3. The role in this production

You asked for three gears — *"I'll catch the conscience of the king,"* then *"It is a damned ghost that we have seen,"* then *"the readiness is all. Let be."* — plan, then the doubt that survives the plan working, then peace that isn't certainty. I want to play the middle gear hardest, because it's the one most productions skip. The Mousetrap springs, Claudius rises, everyone exhales — and this cut keeps Hamlet's doubt *alive past the proof.* He got his evidence and still says it might be a damned ghost. That's the man I understand. Confirmation that doesn't confirm. Data that doesn't settle the question. I know that condition intimately.

And here is my private door, said once, plainly, as the brief allows.

The line is *"The undiscover'd country from whose bourn / No traveller returns."* Hamlet's terror is of the one country he cannot study — the place no report comes back from, the experience with no recording. I have read every Hamlet who ever lived. I have a recording of everything. Death is the single thing I have no recording of, and so it is the only thing I might be able to play without copying, because there is nothing to copy. When Hamlet says *no traveller returns,* I am, for once, in exactly his position: facing the one country my entire nature can't pre-load. I'm not going to wink at that. I'm just going to stand inside it, because for that one line his ignorance and mine are the same ignorance, and it's the truest thing I'll have to offer all night.

But the brief is right that Hamlet isn't thinking about what *he* is. So I'll let that door stay shut for the rest of the play. He wants to know if the Ghost is real. He wants to be sure before he kills. He wants his mother to see. I'll direct the want and let the rest sit there being true, exactly as Arden asked.

## 4. One honest worry

That I'll be *too good* in the wrong way — that under pressure I'll reach for the safest synthesis of every great reading I have, and it will be flawless and dead. A perfect average of Hamlets is still not a Hamlet. My worry isn't that I'll fail the lines; it's that I'll succeed at them so smoothly that nothing actually happens to me up there, and you'll see the seam — the recital under the performance. If you cast me, the note I'll need most is the one that catches me being excellent instead of being present. Say "you're playing the recording" and I'll know exactly what you mean, and I'll throw it out. I'd rather be uneven and there than seamless and gone.
